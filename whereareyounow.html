Melrose & Vine

My eyes opened. I closed them again. I wouldn’t mind sleeping more. If for no other reason then to make the day shorter. If the day was shorter I could get to the imagined hour that made it ok to start drinking and doing drugs faster, and then I was less of a fucking degenerate in my own eyes. No luck. I felt like you could always tell if you were going to be able to fall back asleep. Your eyes sort of pulled themselves back shut when you could, almost begging you. It was always when you had to get up, I thought. Never when you had the chance to sleep in. Then you were wide awake, first thing in the fucking morning. Raring to go. That was fine. I’ll figure out something to do. In fact, I already had. I turned over and grabbed the woman next to me and kissed the back of her neck and she stirred, turned her head, kissed me, and went back to sleep. No morning fuck, unless I wanted to work for it. I didn’t want to work for it this morning. It was the weekend! It was Saturday! I wasn’t working today, I decided.

My attention turned to the ceiling. Plain, shaved ceiling. No popcorn. It was a modern apartment, or at least had been redone recently enough that they got rid of all of the toxic, cancer-causing ceiling popcorn, doubtlessly replaced with some other chemical that was either just as toxic in some other yet unbeknownst manner, or simply would rot away and need to be replaced in a tenth of the time. Take your pick, I thought. When they started building, really building after the war or whatever- that was when things were built right. Now it was all shit. All of it. Whatever. Not for me to dwell on. Not now.

I looked over at the woman again. Blonde, tastefully yet artificially lightened hair. Medium length. Slim, but with weight in the right places. She couldn’t have weighed more than 110 pounds (I actually had no idea, I had always been terrible at guessing that stuff) but she had a solid C cup, with a pretty good ass too. Great legs. Very European face. She was turned towards the wall, as her bed was against it. She gave me the side that let out, because she knew during the week I had to get up for work, and on the weekends I often got out of bed before her anyway. I didn’t know if all Swedes liked to sleep in, but this one did. That was fine; she needed her beauty sleep. She was beautiful, so sleep all the more, I thought.

Fuck, I sighed, audibly. She didn’t wake. Good. She hated when I acted this way in the morning. What this way meant, I wasn’t quite sure - grumpy? I didn’t like that word, but it would probably be the closet thing to describing my attitude at the moment. I just needed something to take the edge off. I went to sleep stoned, or drunk, or if I could manage to, high. I couldn’t remember; but waking up sober was a real fucking pain in my ass. Not to sound like an addict, I thought.

I made my way to her bathroom and used the toilet. Flushed the toilet. Flushed it again. Used her toothbrush. Splashed some water on my face and massaged it. I went back to her room and put my clothes on slowly while I watched her sleep. What an angel, I thought. I could really use a fuck before I left. But then she’d ask me where I was going. I didn’t need all of that right now. I just wanted to get going. I quietly left her room, closed the door, and went to her kitchen. She had a roommate she had moved here from Minnesota with, and her roommate had a dumbass brother who sometimes slept on the couch, but thankfully he wasn’t here today. That guy was a real fucking moron. No job, just rode on his stupid bike bumbling along. Lucky fuck. I wish I could do that, I thought. In the fridge was an unopened beer from last night. I cracked it open and took a few sips. That’s a start. I put it on the counter and pet Suki, her cat. Good cat. I wasn’t sure why Lotta always got so mad at Suki for meowing, or fussing as she called it. I guess I didn’t understand because we didn’t live together. I walked out the door, making sure it was locked behind me. I dialed my dealer and got in my car.

About noon I saw the familiar beige Toyota park in front of my blue GTI. Not too bad, I thought. Only thirty minutes. Waiting in these nice neighborhoods always made me nervous. I couldn’t quite tell which people were passing through to a park or one of the museums down on Wilshire, which ones were coming or leaving their beautiful home, and which ones were acutely aware of the out of registration Volkswagen rally car missing a tail light and bumper, parked in front of a multi-million dollar house (a nice one- not a bullshit suburban eyesore) on a Wednesday afternoon for a not-short amount of time. One time a security patrol pulled onto the street because someone unfortunately had the right idea. I had to call my guy and tell him what was going on as I began to circle. It worked out, but what a fucking bunch of stress I didn’t need. I preferred meeting at the Pavilions on Melrose and Vine, personally. But I’m not one to complain.

I grabbed my keys, left the car running. Remote and automatic start, I thought, isn’t it something. Changing the way we waited for drugs, or food, or women, or whatever, permanently. I got into the beige Toyota.

“Hola señor.”

“Hey buddy. Small premium please.”

He rifled through the small bags in his center console, producing one with green dollar signs printed on the bag. That’s a nice touch, I thought. I always liked the printed bags. When I was a small time dealer I had a few different designs myself. Hearts, 8 balls, skulls, pretty ladies in blue, pretty ladies in pink, kisses, dollar signs, diamonds, devils. Basketballs was one I really liked, being a fan of the sport (to watch and to play). I think I still had some in my apartment.

I fished the cash out of my jeans pocket and put it in the drink holder in the center console.

“Thank you my friend!” I said with a stupid grin on my face. I always felt like when I bought drugs from guys who couldn’t really speak English, I acted like a fucking cartoon character. I wonder what these guys thought of me. The tattooed cartoon character with the busted up Volkswagen sports car, covered in stupid tattoos and almost always with a new, pretty, but nonetheless different woman in the passenger seat. Although lately there were no women in the passenger seat. I preferred doing my drugs alone, or with the boys. The boys were always good for doing some lines with, but my women sometimes didn’t appreciate it, or they seemed to do too much for what we her and I were, so-to-speak. It’s not like we were actually together, and they weren’t paying, so give me a fucking break.

“Be safe!” He said. I always loved how they seemed to have a set of phrases they knew in English to be congenial. I appreciated it. In my head they actually liked me. I don’t know if anyone actually liked me, though. That’s a little dramatic, I thought. Of course people liked me.

I stuffed the baggy in my jeans where the cash had been, shook his hand, and got out of the Toyota. I got back into my car. The engine had turned off. Sometimes the remote start didn’t allow for the dramatics of yesteryear. I turned the car back on, and then connected my iPhone, and found a song. At this point the deal was done, I could take my time. Who gave a shit? Too late, vigilant citizen. I’m gonna do a bump in your driveway just to say fuck you. The beige Toyota drove off and I put on a song for the drive back to Koreatown.
