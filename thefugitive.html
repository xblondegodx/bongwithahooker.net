U.S. Detective Marshal-Surgeon Richard Kimball, A Review of The Fugitive (1993)

Right off the bat the cast card going TOMMY LEE before flipping to JONES is absolutely crazy. I mean that’s genuinely awesome. I need to try and find that to put here because everyone has to see that. That is so fucking good. I guess it would have looked ridiculous no matter how it was framed, but that’s what they chose? Ok. Moving on.

I definitely thought this movie would be, for lack of a better word, complete schlock. You know, fun, good, but total schlock. And there’s nothing wrong with that- the 90s was a great era for schlock. But maybe this movie is schlock and the shit back then was just better than it is now. I could definitely believe that. But maybe it’s also ridiculous to think a movie with the following actors could be shit:

- Harrison Ford


- Tommy Lee…Jones


- Joe Pantoliano (Any other Matrix fans?)


- Julianne Moore (!?)


- Jane Lynch (?!!)


- Neil Flynn (included for a reason, see immediate following side note)

[Side note: the famous scene where Neil Flynn catches Kimball before getting blown away by Sykes on the train! Made famous by Scrubs, where The Janitor is in fact the man in The Fugitive… this is really a great bit.]

This is basically an ensemble cast in the 90s. Harrison Ford and Tommy Lee Jones alone. Tommy Lee Jones was doing serious work, he definitely ran the 90s. That voice is iconic. That being said, a lot of movies in the 80s/90s were dog shit! How many B movies are from the 80s? How many corny ass movies were made in the 90s? I guess B movies and corny movies are different now, but those are separate pieces to write another time.

My point being when it comes to schlock and corn, this is not one of them or at least not one of the bad ones- despite the premise seeming a little light on first glance. On the contrary, what a plot- advancing from a shoot-em-up (the crack house bit that introduces us to his U.S. Marshals squad is hilarious) to cop chase to (political) thriller is pretty fucking good. More importantly, The Fugitive is also an excellent look at institutional failures that interfere with individual competence. How a man would have to take on not just a large city’s police force, but the federal marshal service, in order to find the man who actually killed his wife and clear his name. How’s that for a plot?

I really do love that Agent Gerard and his team seem to understand there’s something up and that maybe Kimball is not totally full of shit with all his insane running around trying to prove who actually killed his wife. The fact that he didn’t just take off is pretty odd! Considering they definitely never would have fucking found him. I mean Kimball is doing Gerard’s fucking job for him- and Chicago PD’s for that matter. The scene where Gerard and co. gather the detectives from Kimball’s murder case is great. Just the dumbest assholes you can imagine investigating a murder. “Well, you heard the 911 call! What’s all this about a one-armed man? Some surgeon nonsense, surely. Anyway, off to prison!” The cop saying “Well… his wife had more money” when being confronted with the fact that doctors make a lot of money is great. That being said, earlier when Gerard first confronts Kimball he tells him he does not care if he killed his wife or not. Which, yeah. That sounds about right for the American justice system, certainly the U.S. Marshal Service part of it. They just wanna play cowboys. But nonetheless, Gerard and his team begin investigating and eventually stumble upon a strange plot …

And what a great plot it is! A multi-billion dollar (“$7.2 billion… that’s a killing.”) pharmaceutical company’s major breakthrough/money-making drug is actually destroying people’s livers and they are snuffing out anyone who dare speak out against it. Whether it be a fatal car crash (Aubrey McClendon?) or cancer (!!!???) they will get rid of their enemies. You know, that’s pretty fucking believable! I could see Purdue perpetrating some similar sort of evil, couldn’t you? Also, of course Nichols is a fucking backstabber. I mean naturally. Slightly foreign accent, strangely helpful, weird. All the hallmark tenets of a backstabber, really. Also, him giving the speech and seeing Kimball walk up in the crowd before going “dishonest- er, honest” is such a good bit. I mean what is that, a “Freudian slip”? Really good shit here.

But seriously, why does Nichols help Kimball? This is probably the most unbelievable part of the movie. I mean Sykes is taking out everyone who dares fuck with the company and their money-maker drug, and has killed Kimball’s wife and framed him for her murder. Ok, so he’s gone now. Not the tied knot I would totally expect since these guys seem to be capable of arranging fatal car crashes, but nonetheless. It’s certainly dramatic! Does Nichols think that this will help the cover up of his having killed Lentz? I don’t think anyone was on to you: Kimball’s off to jail, Sykes is off scot-free, and here you are giving a keynote speech for the very fucking drug! And yet here he goes, stirring shit up like a dumbass. Kimball is doing the work for him! Well, Sykes is. And Kimball raving about a one-armed man is only helping! I mean what is this, Twin Peaks? Haha, just kidding- although it is a funny coincidence that the one-armed man from Twin Peaks is named Gerard… anyway. I guess you were right Nichols, Kimball is smarter than you!

An excellent fight scene ensues between Gerard, Kimball, and Nichols through the hotel and culminates with- you guessed it- Kimball once again doing Gerard’s fucking job for him. I mean God damn dude. You are being completely outclassed by this fucking surgeon in detective work and now at just like, saving mother fucking lives and taking out bad guys. Surgeon-Marshal Kimball! Gerard, no titles! Not to disrespect Tommy Lee Jones like that. This character is just such a bitch-ass, honestly. Kimball saves Gerard; Nichols and Sykes are arrested. Excellent.

In absolutely hilarious press conference, reporters ask the same stupid fucking Chicago PD detectives from earlier the following-

“He said the murderer was a one-armed man and now you have a one-armed man in custody, is that true?”

“Just how many one-armed men are there in Chicago?”

This comedic relief is not lost on me. Thank you to the screenwriters.

The movie’s final scene of dialogue takes place between Kimball and Gerard in the latter’s car. In a truly touching scene, Gerard reveals to Kimball that- you know what? He does care that Kimball didn’t kill his wife. … What the fuck? This is a bizarrely blunt admission, showing that only when the system is forced to capitulate and admit fault will it, and even then, Gerard says not to tell anyone. Naturally! A perfect metaphor for the government silencing the truth. Really though, what an insane thing to say. Honestly I wish Nichols had killed you, stupid fucking asshole. But then we wouldn’t get to see the adventures of U.S. Marshal Gerard in what I did not realize was a sequel, U.S. Marshals, which I will undoubtedly be watching soon.

3/5.
